Legal · Part 1 of 2

Important legal notes

When you operate the system you are personally responsible for compliance with all legal requirements. Please read these points carefully.

If you operate a contact form or qualification form you are required to inform users about the processing of their data. Your privacy policy must include: what you do with the data, who processes it (including any data processors), how long you store it and how someone can request deletion.

Use a reputable generator such as eRecht24 or Datenschutz.org and have the statement reviewed by a lawyer. A missing or outdated privacy policy can lead to cease-and-desist letters.

Before submitting a form, the user must actively consent to their data being processed. This means: a checkbox that the user must tick themselves – no pre-ticked box, no opt-out.

The checkbox must link to the privacy policy and clearly state what the consent is being given for. Document these consents in case of subsequent queries.

Promotional emails and follow-up emails that do not directly relate to the original enquiry require an explicit opt-in. This applies to all automated emails that do not directly answer the enquiry – such as nurturing sequences or newsletters.

Add a separate, voluntary checkbox in the qualification form with the exact content of the communication you intend to send. This checkbox must not be a required field.

Calls and WhatsApp messages for advertising purposes without express consent are legally problematic in Germany and can give rise to injunction claims. This also applies to automated WhatsApp messages via Superchat or similar tools.

Collect consent in the form with a separate checkbox for telephone and another for WhatsApp. Document when and by whom consent was given – also for future proof if needed.

Legal · Part 2 of 2

Contracts, data storage & legal notice

If you use tools such as Make.com, Google (Workspace, Forms, Drive), Superchat, ElevenLabs or Cloudinary and process personal data in doing so, you need a data processing agreement (DPA) with each of these providers.

Most providers make the DPA available automatically as part of their terms of use or on request for download. Check in your account settings whether a DPA has been concluded, or conclude one actively. Without a DPA you may not process personal data with these tools.

You may not store personal data indefinitely. The GDPR requires a clear purpose limitation – once the purpose ceases the data must be deleted.

Recommendation for a simple deletion concept: delete prospects assessed as unsuitable after 6 months. Delete prospects who no longer respond or react to any communication after 12 months. Document your deletion concept in writing – this protects you in the event of enquiries from the data protection authority.

Every commercially used website and every commercially used social media profile requires a complete legal notice (Impressum). This also applies to Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn and TikTok profiles you use as part of this system.

The legal notice must contain: full name or company name, address, contact details (email and telephone), and for limited companies the trade register number and managing directors. A missing or incomplete legal notice is a common cease-and-desist risk. Use a legal notice generator and have it checked.
